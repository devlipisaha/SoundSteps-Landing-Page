How it works

Five simple steps to meaningful practice

Every session follows a clear, encouraging structure — so your child knows what to expect and parents feel confident guiding the practice.

🔊
1

Listen to a sound

The app plays a clear audio example of the target speech sound for your child to hear.

🗣️
2

Child repeats the sound

Your child repeats the sound, practising with simple, age-appropriate words and prompts.

💛
3

Parent encourages

Gentle on-screen prompts help parents give the right encouragement at the right moment.

🎤
4

Recordings are saved

Optional voice recordings capture your child's progress over time for you and your therapist.

📈
5

Gentle suggestions

SoundSteps suggests what to practise next based on the session, keeping progress on track.

Why we're building SoundSteps

A tool born from our own family's experience

SoundSteps began from our own family's experience. Our eldest child struggled with speech development. When he started nursery many people struggled to understand him.

Over time his closest friends learned to understand what he was saying — sometimes even helping teachers interpret his speech at school. Although he received some early support, speech development has remained something we continue to work on even now.

Our younger child is now facing similar challenges, but accessing support has become even more difficult. Waiting lists are longer, appointments are often cancelled or rearranged, and families can spend long periods unsure how best to help their child practise at home.

This experience led us to start building SoundSteps — a simple tool designed to help families practise speech sounds regularly at home while waiting for, or alongside, professional support.
